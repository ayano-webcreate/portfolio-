Concept

"Between the lines, silence speaks.
Between the forms, truth waits."

HARU invites you to explore the invisible — the tension between what is drawn and what is left undrawn, between presence and absence. These works are not finished. They await your gaze to complete them.

Working across drawing, painting, and installation, this exhibition traces a decade of searching — for the moment before meaning, for the space where a line becomes a breath.

HARU

Contemporary Visual Artist  ·  Tokyo

HARU is a contemporary visual artist whose practice spans drawing, painting, and large-scale installation. Born in Kyoto, based in Tokyo, their work investigates the poetics of restraint — the line as breath, the void as language, the unfinished as an act of courage.

Drawn to the charged space between intention and accident, HARU constructs works that resist completion, inviting the viewer's gaze to activate meaning. The result is a body of work that is simultaneously intimate and architectural, quiet and demanding.
